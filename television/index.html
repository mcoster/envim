Updates on past posts, and other assorted tidbits

I'm long overdue to update on a few past posts, so will do so today:

  • I finally got around to conducting the "de-stink Pinky" test. I spritzed Pinky with a half/half mix of vinegar/water and PH12.6. PH12.6 worked instantly and left no residual smell. The vinegar water worked but left a residual smell for a while, but then dissipated. This is also one instance where procrastination worked in my favor as untreated areas seem to have lost the campfire smell just from airing out over time.
  • I made the soft butter using a hand-mixer instead of a stand-mixer with no problems and thumbs up from the family. I am not a kitchen whiz, but this was very easy, and no one seems to note any difference in taste from the store-bought in-a-throwaway-plastic-container version. Yay! There's one less plastic item I need to recycle!
  • This whole thing about reuse of "disposable" baby wipes is beyond cool. Sometimes when I write a post, I'm mostly just trying to be funny and then end up learning something amazing like this. I don't use a lot of baby wipes, but still have enough around, and am thrilled to be tossing them into the hamper for a wash now instead of in the trash. I even made sure during a family discussion last night that everyone understood I was throwing these in the hamper with a purpose, and for them not to think ol' mom has really lost it and is throwing garbage in the hamper.
  • Cheap-o-Dad continues to ride his bike to work. His new goal is to not gas-up again until Thanksgiving. I am trying not to freak about the horrific recent spate of bike v. truck fatalities in Portland. If there's any good in it, it's that some real discussion has been going on regarding making the roads safer for bike riders.
  • OPB will feature a special on The Silence of the Bees this Sunday on Nature from 8-9pm. Looks very interesting and worth viewing or taping. Did you know honeybees are responsible for every third bite we eat?

Enviro friendly kid shows

Becky recently emailed with a tip on the Disney Channel's Charlie and Lola show about recycling:

"Hi, I was looking at my tivo list and noticed a special Charlie and Lola on Recycling and saving the planet. It was all broken down really easy for kids and was only about 15 min long. I don't know if you could find it to purchase yet. I just thought that you might be interested since you like to help us all find great ways to teach our children about being a little more green."

I've never seen Charlie and Lola, but it looks darling (we don't get the Disney Channel). We have gone through a major Bob the Builder phase, and have learned from a few of their enviro friendly shows:

  • Bob saves the porcupines
  • Bob and the badgers
  • Bob's big plan (the city is taking bids to develop Sunflower Valley and Bob comes up the genius plan to stop development of Sunflower Valley and leave it pristine, beautiful and untouched -- ah, if only real life were so simple!!)

Heather borrowed a copy of Adventures with Wink and Blink: A Day in the Life of a Garbage Truck from the library, and she said her kids enjoyed it.

What other kids shows have you watched with your kids that help spread the message of going green? If you're not into TV and videos, we posted a list of enviro friendly kids books for your reference.

Happy Earth Day - 2007

Happy Earth Day, everyone! And thank you Oprah, for airing Friday's "going green 101" show. If there's anyone who can influence the masses to make positive changes, it's Oprah. If you missed the show, they covered a lot of the basics: switching to reusable shopping bags, changing to CFL lightbulbs, stopping junk mail, recycling, conserving energy, using non-toxic cleaning products, buying local and organic food, cutting down on paper waste (napkins, etc.). Her goal was to inspire each viewer to make one small change. That goal is a lot like ours. Take baby steps. Make positive changes. They do add up. And you'll feel good about it.

One positive change I'm working on is making sure paper is used on both sides before it is recycled. My kids love to do artwork. This is the "earth day" card my 5-year-old made for me. Consequently, I am setting up an filing area in our playroom so we have a keeping place for scrap paper, we can find it when an artistic urge strikes (and I don't overzealously throw it into the recycle bin before we've used it on both sides). I have a weakness regarding clutter. If things look too messy, I tend to want to pitch it and just be rid of it. But alas, I've recently toured recycling facilities and the dump. And I'm amping up my efforts to reduce/reuse these days.

What about you? Did Oprah, your GreenGroup or EnviroMom inspire you to make a small green change? If so, what is it? We'd love to hear about it. Happy Earth Day everyone!

How many enviroMoms does it take to change a light bulb?

What do Leonardo DiCaprio, Julia Roberts, Al Gore, Oprah, Heather and I have in common? I wish the answer was that we are all getting together for a wine- tasting this weekend, but that would be a lie. The boring but true answer is that we care about the environment. For those of us who have kids, we want there to be a living planet for our children and our children's children.

I just got finished watching Global Warming 101 (yesterday's Oprah show featuring Al Gore). He talks about his book and movie An Inconvenient Truth and the things we can do to make a difference. Here are five simple things you can do to slow down global warming:

  • Purchase compact fluorescent bulbs
  • Insulate/blanket your electric hot water heater (there are specially made blankets available from home improvement stores)
  • Change your air filters in your heating/cooling systems
  • Use a programmable thermostat and adjust temps a few degrees higher in warm months and cooler in cold months (my husband takes this one way too seriously - you don't want to take you coat off when you visit our house this winter!)
  • Use solar lights for landscaping

The book and DVD of An Inconvenient Truth are available for purchase -  or at the very least a rental or borrow from the library. I confess there are times when I feel overwhelmed by the problems of our world. I also get overwhelmed by the questions my daughters ask me on a simple car drive: Why did you just stop? What are angels? Why do swipers take things that aren't theirs?

My point is that there is something we can do about global warming. I may never be able to answer all my daughters questions and be able to drive safely at the same time.
