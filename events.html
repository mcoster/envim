Best Mother's Day Ever


Last year on Mother's Day, a group of some of my favorite moms met to go for a nature walk in a nearby state park with our families. We all got together at our local neighborhood elementary school. We had our hiking shoes, our sunscreen, our hats and water bottles. And a nearby playground. The kids went off to do the monkey bars, and play in that ideal way where there's no fights big enough to necessitate parent mediation. The parents found an inviting picnic table in a sunny spot, and some great conversation. Needless to say, the nature walk never happened, but hands down, this was one of the best Mother's Day celebrations ever. No gifts. No big expectations. Just the company and companionship of some of my best peeps.

Mother's Day is this Sunday. How will you celebrate? What was your best Mother's Day ever?

Post-Holiday Recycling at Portland area Whole Foods stores

Here's an important date to put on your calendar for the new year: January 8, 2011. Why? Whole Foods stores in the Portland Metro area will host post-holiday recycling events to take all that non-curbside recycling off your hands. You know the kind of stuff festive merry-making produces: rigid plastics, electronics, styrofoam, and even clothing and household items too. Friends of Tryon Creek Holiday Forest Market: Dec 4-5

Holiday lights up? Nope.

Out before the break of dawn on Black Friday, throwing elbows, kicking shins to get the best shopping deals? Nah.

Sent out all the holiday cards moments after the Thanksgiving turkey was made into soup? As if...

I've done none of those things! I don't even have good intentions in many of those areas. I still have a string of lights from last Christmas sitting on my workbench, if only I could figure out how to repair it. Shopping retail? Believe it or not, I need to get working on some of the home-made gifts my kids asked for this year. And, as for holiday cards? Well, you should know by now we don't send those. We make a holiday video that is paper free. We've begun production. It will be epic. There will be elves. And that's all I'm going to say about that for now.

So, getting into the holiday spirit could just happen next weekend at the Friends of Tryon Creek Holiday Forest Market. Here's the scoop (and it sounds quite lovely):

Artisans from the Portland area will be selling their beautiful handmade, sustainable and crafty wares (from delicious organic chocolate truffles and lush holiday wreaths to hand-thrown pottery and decorative art made of reclaimed wood, there truly will be something for everyone on your readers’ lists). There will also be local authors on hand to discuss their books on hiking and camping in our area, as well as children’s literature. All books will be available for purchase.

The Forest Market is a fun, free, family event. It is held in the Nature Center of beautiful Tryon Creek State Natural Area, right in our own back yard. In addition to the market, there will be a free guided nature hike at 10am on Saturday, December 4, and free holiday crafts for children from 1 – 3pm on Sunday, December 5.

Portland Fix-It Fairs coming up!

This is the time of year when I wish I was a renter. Then I could just call up the landlord and give him/her a piece of my mind:

Yes, hello? Just wondering when you're going to come and caulk the windows we talked about? Don't you remember? The ones that were leaking from the rain last winter. I told you about them. October 6, 2010: International Walk + Bike Day rocks!

Weather deities smiled upon Portland for International Walk + Bike to school day. Great turnout. My six-year-old summed it up succinctly: 'Walk day is fun!' Well, except for Heather's son, who accidentally stepped in dog poop.

Why do we walk or bike or board or scoot?

  • Green and human powered school trips lessen traffic congestion. Did you know that 20 to 25% of morning traffic is adults driving children to school? Ack!
  • It improves air quality. Exhaust from motor vehicles is the largest source of air pollution around schools.
  • It contributes to student health. Human powered trips to school on a regular basis reduce obesity rates and lessen the risk for heart disease and depression. Plus it's just darn fun to see all your peeps first thing in the morning.
  • It improves academic performance by helping students concentrate and do better in school. Nothing like a little bracing autumn air to wake your mind before that school bell rings.
  • It increases student safety. Here's a scary one -- 50% of children hit by cars near schools involve cars driven by parents of other students.

When the kids got to school, there were some tokens to acknowledge their efforts and a poster to sign. Love that!

10:00 AM - 2:00 PM
Community Health Education Center
Legacy Meridian Park Medical Center
19300 SW 65th Avenue
Tualatin, OR 97062
Cost/Fee: Free

Bicycle and multi-sport helmets will be available for a reduced rate of $5 (cash or check only) including fitting. Kids can bring in their own helmets for correct fitting and adjustments. Free car safety seat checks conducted by Certified Passenger Safety Technicians. Recycle your used, outdated or damaged car safety seats. And what with the economy, there are very few openings for fish rehab. Only the rich fish get the help they need. But they have to really, really want to get well. You know how it goes.

Therefore, we do heartily encourage you to take advantage of the U.S. Drug Enforcement Administration's first national prescription drug event (whew! that's a mouthful!), coming up this Saturday, September 25.
