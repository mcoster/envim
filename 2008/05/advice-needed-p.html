Advice needed: pest management in organic garden

It's not news to anyone that I have not the first clue what I'm doing in my garden. Just tryin' to grow a few fruits and veggies and flowers. Organic-like. Chemical free. Now, don't be alarmed. I'm not armed with Roundup in hand ready to blast away.

But SOMEONE or SOMETHING has been eating my strawberry.



And SOMEONE or SOMETHING has been eating my daphne.

And SOMEONE or SOMETHING has been eating my oregano... Well, maybe just a little bit. Tiny nibbles. On one leaf. Maybe two. If you look closely enough. On second thought, I guess the oregano doesn't look half bad.

So here's the thing. I have no idea what that SOMEONE or SOMETHING may be. It doesn't seem to be slug, the main nemesis in our garden of past years. I spy no slug trails. Is it even possible for a corpulent slug to climb up a waif-like little oregano without knocking the poor plant down? What to do, farmers? What to do? The truth is, I have four strawberry plants, and only one has these nibbles on it. I have a whole bunch of other herbs, and this is the only one with bites on it. The daphne -- there's only one of those. Do I ignore? And hope the pest goes away? Do I spray it with some lemon juice or vinegar or organic-y stuff? Should I call 1-800-MASTER-GARDNER? Just don't know what to do here except worry myself silly over my little fledgling plants...

If it is slugs, which would be my guess, diotomaceous earth works well, but has to be reapplied after a rain. Use beer in shallow containers. The slugs climb in and drown themselves. It works well. Also I've used dish soap diluted with water and sprayed on the plants-- takes care of aphids, etc.

Posted by: Michele | May 19, 2008 at 09:43 PM

I have a wonderful book called the Organic Gardener by Catharine Osgood Foster. It's a bit dated but the info is awesome. I, too, am a incredibly novice gardener, especially when it comes to organic and anti chemical gardening. However, this book is FILLED with info on what kinds of plants to plant together to attract the right kinds of creatures, and which to plant to scare them off. Lots of good ideas, I haven't been able to read in depth yet, so I can't be of too much assistance, but if you can get ahold of this book (Amazon) I know it would be an invaluable tool! One trick that I've used thus far is to leave a bowl of sweetened water covered with mesh in the corner of my garden. Small insects that eat my plants are drawn to it, and then drown in it!

Posted by: Lacey | May 19, 2008 at 10:06 PM

I also have a book recommendation. Check out Great Garden Companions: A Companion-Planting System for a Beautiful, Chemical-Free Vegetable Garden by Sally Jean Cunningham. It's full of great advice about what works well with what to keep pests at bay and make your garden as productive as possible. The lay-out of the book is very logical and it's an easy read, which I wouldn't say of most of my gardening books. I think most of the advice would work well even if you weren't planting a veggie garden.

Added bonus: it's printed in the US on acid-free, recycled paper. It's vegan, as it were!!

Posted by: laney | May 20, 2008 at 09:59 AM

Beer trapping is quite effective, but caused a little ruckus as our house. Possums love slugs and once they find the traps they will frequently check back for their favorite little treats. Not a problem unless you have pets who think possums look an awful lot like stuffed animals. We now only beer trap in the front yard.

I've also had success with Dr. Bronner's and water (2 tsp per 16 ounces) and diatomaceous earth.

Posted by: Carrie | May 20, 2008 at 10:46 AM

Definitely try diotomaceous earth. Will not only take care of slugs, will take care of any low-to-the-ground insects. Wear gloves to apply (can be an eye/lung irritant), apply when no wind, sprinkle to create perimeter lines all the way around your plants with a cup or a funnel (don't put ON plants). Funnel is best, as it allows you to create precise lines, not waste product. Just place your finger under the funnel to stop/start the flow. Whoever they are, I hope you are able to get rid of them! :)
