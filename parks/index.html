Natural discovery garden & worm composting @ Blue Lake

Blue Lake Regional Park now boasts a natural discovery garden located next to the water spray ground. Here's what the latest Metro Solid Waste and Recycling Newsletter has to say about it:

Children and grownups can experience the wonders of nature while learning how to garden naturally and organically. The garden is loaded with many plants to see, smell and touch. Many of them are native to the region, like huckleberry and coastal strawberry. Others include non-invasive exotics like chocolate cosmos and pineapple sage. All of the plants were selected to require little, if any, water, fertilizers and pesticides. The garden also features a compost exploration area, a digging area and a worm bin. In addition, two rain gardens capture water from the roof of a log gazebo to prevent pollution in our storm drain system and local rivers. The gazebo provides seating and shade, and large boulders and logs offer fun climbing opportunities for kids.

In addition, Metro is offering a free Worm Composting seminar this Saturday, August 18 at Blue Lake from 11am to noon. Children are welcome to attend, but call Metro to register first: 503-234-3000.

Here's a great invitation from Three Rivers Land Conservancy for National Family Recreation Day (June 2):

"Hey Enviromoms - My name is Melanie and I am the volunteer coordinator for Three Rivers Land Conservancy.  I heard about your group through a variety of people here at our offices.  I don't know the age of most of you kids, but I am putting together an event for June 2nd at our Keller Woodlands location.  June 2nd is National Family Recreation Day and I thought it would be great to get families to come out and help us restore some land.  Why not clean up the areas your family plays! The forests of Portland are places for families to hike, run, walk and teach each other about the importance of nature, keep them healthy for future generations!   The work parties run from 9am-12pm and the site is located .6 miles north of the Chart House on Terwilliger Blvd.  There are pull outs on both sides of the street for parking, look for the West Willamette Restoration banner and the Three Rivers Land Conservancy banner.  Let me know if anyone from your group would be interested in helping us out.  The weather should be good, and it is a nice way to spend a Saturday morning."

Festival of the Birds @ Sellwood Park May 12

We've got a little robin nesting in our vine maple, just about eye level. My kids want to check on her hourly to make sure that the evil blue jays haven't come calling. I don't know a whole lot about birds (in fact, I'm not really sure it's a robin) so this weekend's Festival of Birds might be a good place to learn a little more. The festival celebrates migratory birds in our area and offers a host of activities for kids like bird feeder building, make your own binoculars, storytelling and the opportunity to see the Audubon Society's resident birds of prey up close. May 12, 10am-4pm @ Sellwood Park. Free!

Hey Rover, meet me over at the doggie loo in 5 minutes

Have you heard about the Gabriel Park doggie loo? It's funny because I had been thinking about what happens to doggie doo a lot lately. We do not have a dog, but of course since Portland is dog-town USA, we always see dog walkers with their ubiquitous plastic poop bags in their hands. And it got me to wondering what they do with those bags...

To give you a little background, let me take you back 5 years to the infant care class I took at Providence St. Vincent when my first daughter was due. The biggest thing I got out of that class was a real shocker. Apparently, there is fine print on the disposable diaper boxes that says you should shake the baby poop out of the diaper and flush it in the toilet before disposing of the soiled diaper.  Now I can't say that I've done that with every single diaper. But I've made a heroic effort of this dirty job lately. My toddler has taken a great interest in potty training, and now we always flush the poop, so that she makes the connection that poop goes in the potty.

So that brings me back to the dog poop. Now I want you to consider a truly awesome figure: 5 tons That is the amount of dog poop that was going in trash bins at Gabriel Park each summer prior to the installation of the dog loo. Then garbage trucks were taking those stinky loads to our landfills. Not a good place for raw sewage.

Let's do our part. Go that extra mile. Put poop in it's place. No if's, and's or but's about it. But be sure and wash your hands really well afterwards.

Ladybug Nature Hikes by Portland Parks and Recreation

I took the kids to our first Ladybug Hike a few weeks ago at Elk Rock Island in Milwaukie. Never heard of it? Neither had I, but it's fantastic. The guides passed out ladybug backpacks chock full with magnifying glasses, specimen cups, animal masks (for getting into character) and other goodies appropriate for a walk in nature. There were lots of big rocks that the kids had to climb over to get through the hike, but they did great and we all had fun. I knew it was a success when my daughter, who is usually squeamish about insects, mollusks and the like, threw a tantrum when she couldn't stay longer and catch more snails.

There are only seven hikes left this year, so try to get to one! The next hike is this Friday, September 22 from 10 - 11:30 at Leach Botanical Garden, and it costs $2 per child. If you are considering bringing a baby jogger, check the hike schedule to see if the trail is stroller-friendly.
