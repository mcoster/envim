Savoring summer

I'm starting to wonder where the time went. Here it is. The middle of summer. Or even later, depending on when your kids go back to school!

We've been busy with the normal summer activities. Picking berries at our favorite u-pick farms. Rummage sale-ing. Camping with friends. Swim lessons. Watching the neighbors' chickens. Walking, biking, and scootering around the 'hood.

It doesn't seem so long ago that the kids were in school, it was cold and rainy and I was fiercely anticipating the arrival of summer. Not just the fun summer happenings. But the new life budding out of trees, poking out of the ground, the colors and the good smells. Such as this beautiful dogwood bloom (and just WHY do they call it a dogwood? It doesn't look anything like a dog. In fact, it's quite pretty.)


Yet, amazingly, soon after the blossoms come, the leaves start to fade (note the bloom in the background)! It strikes me how fleeting the time is, and how it needs to be savored as best we can. We just spent a week with Heather's family, as they visited our great Northwest. It was wonderful for us to re-connect, yet sad to see them go. So we go back to contenting ourselves with all the good stuff of NW summers. Spending as much time as possible outdoors. I hope you all are doing the same.

Outdoor kids: conduct a worm census


Nine out of 10 EnviroKids say: 'Worms are slimy and awesome!' If you are just entering the unstructured days of spring break as we are, and find that spring has sprung, try taking a worm census. We've noticed a bumper crop of worms on our daily walks to school -- you pretty much can't take a step without stepping on a red wiggler in our neck of the woods! My kids like nothing better than having an important job, such as counting worms on a walk or during a garden planting. Encourage your kids to conduct a worm census. Take pictures! Share said pictures and census results with us here on EnviroMom! Because EnviroKids are right. Worms are slimy and awesome.

Walking to school: 'Just try to keep up, mom!'

I am all for walking to school. Especially on sunny, warm, dry days that come once or twice a year in the form of International Walk + Bike to School Day. Otherwise, I've been pretty happy to have my kids ride that big yellow school bus. Because:

1) The kids have almost always enjoyed the fun atmosphere of the bus, such that it has been like a rolling playdate

2) Riding the school bus is good preparation for using mass transit

3) It amounts to a full hour a free day care every day (selfish! awful! I know!!)

So when my kids spontaneously decided last weekend that they wanted to walk to and from school every day of the year from here on out forever and ever amen. I was like: whoa. Because do you know what that means? I will not be able to wave from the front window to my kids on the bus (whilst still in my slippers and PJs holding a cup of java). It means I will really need to start waking up earlier, even thought it's winter and daybreak still comes late and most mornings I feel like a hibernating bear. It means I can't linger over the morning paper as I have for the past several years.

But if there's one thing I've learned as a parent, nothing is as golden as when your kids set a healthy goal for themselves and just pounce. Thus I am trying to kick my sorry behind into gear and keep up. We've so far enjoyed the fresh air, and sociability of walking with friends every morning and afternoon. It's yet to rain on our parade. Change never comes easy, but I'm EnviroMom, right? I'd be an idiot to pass on this walking opportunity even if it means my day just got a lot shorter. Just try not to mention it if you happen to notice I'm wearing my PJs under my coat, ok?

Families that play together (outside), stay together (at least when it's sunny)

Portland has been a pretty happy place lately. It's not just that we've suddenly been thrust into the spotlight via the IFC show 'Portlandia,' so that the whole world can laugh with us at our tree-hugging, organic-loving, grown-up game-playing, eco-freaky ways.

It's that it has been sunny and dry for almost a whole week! Last time that happened, it was summer! Well not really, but it sure feels like it.

I hate to admit to being such a fair-weather groupie. I know I do not represent all Portlanders. There are those who are out every day, no matter what the weather. And I do go out almost every day. Just not with my kids. And not as happily as this past week.

So this sun break has been great for us. It's allowed for a whole lot of hopscotch playing in our driveway. Finally, a game I can school my kids at. The homework is completed briskly because we want to roll the rocks onto squares and hop from space to space without touching the lines.

Is hopscotch just a girl game? I can't remember if I ever played it with boys. I suppose it'd be just as easy to chalk up a game of four square.

So happy Friday everyone. I hope you have chalk and a game to play with your green kids. Just don't join a grown-up hide-n-seek league. That's so Portlandia.

Outdoor kids: taking a color seeking walk

Gray is the predominant color of winter here in Portland. The rain, the defoliated trees, the soggy ground, and the general mood. A neighbor recently described the gloom: like living at the bottom of a pond. Apt. It also feels like winter has been going on for-ev-er. And it's still just January.

So on Saturday, on what was quite possibly the nicest day of the year so far, our family set off on a big walk around our neighborhood. Out to prove that gray is not the only color. Our color walk was a major success. Look at the natural beauty we found, starting with some red buds:

Orange-red berries!

Bright yellow-green leaves!

Yellow blossoms!

OK, now. These plastic purple forks don't really count. But it never fails to give me a smile when I see this plastic cutlery marking where bulbs were planted. Bonus points for creative reuse!

Thank you blue sky for getting us outside. Yet, I believe most of these same colors could be spotted, no matter how gray the day. And as is often the case when getting out of doors, we came home much happier than we started.

Camping rocks!

The key to a successful camping trip is planning ahead. And a lack of mosquitos. And lots of cold beer. It also helps to camp with friends, as we will soon be. Six families. All the kids and grown-ups are connected via our elementary school. I have been looking forward to this camping trip since we reserved our campsites months ago. In many ways, camping is the highlight of our summer.

So why is it that being covered with layers of sunscreen, bug juice and campfire smoke, coupled with not bathing for several days and having to put up with pit toilets, is so enjoyable? I really cannot explain.

Last year, all of our kids whizzed around the camp site loop on their bikes with joyful glee. Oh, there were crashes. Sure, but nothing serious. There was a hike in the woods where we sampled wild thimble berries. In past years, highlights included peaceful outings in inflatable boats on the gorgeous Lake TImothy, with Mt. Hood looking down on us as in blessing. This year, there's a possibility we'll witness some of the Perseids meteor shower, as we did several years ago in the crystal clear skies above the national forest. The stunning beauty of nature explains it in part. Factor in hanging out by the camp fire once all the kids are tucked in their sleeping bags, just talking and laughing with all the grownups. Life really doesn't get any better than that.


So today, I'm baking banana bread. Much easier to cook now, and just eat and enjoy while there. And grocery shopping. I've also been freezing giant blocks of ice to keep our cooler cold (although I'm open to any tips, as we've never been very successful at keeping the beer cold enough). And also wondering just what the heck is the deal with those GINORMOUS marshmallows on sale this year? They are about four times the size of regular marshmallows. Marshmallows in bulk? A candidate for DudeThatsNotFood.com? Or just a newfangled way to supersize us all?

Do you camp? What are your best camping tips (especially if it involves keeping beer very, very cold)? And your best camping memories?

Outdoor kids: finding snake skins and slug races

Welcome to the middle of summer! How did that happen? In some ways, it's going so fast I can't believe it. Other days, I start to wonder why it is that my kids think they need to follow me to the bathroom and be with me every single minute of every day and how I've managed to keep my sanity thus far.


The short answer? The great outdoors. My kids are better people when they play outside. The hula hoops and jump ropes have seen a lot of action Then there's the nature appreciation stuff. We've found two snake skins, which have become hallowed treasures. And the kids have also debuted a new game: snake slithering out of its skin. That's a good one for a really hot day when you've been out all morning, and it's time to retreat to your blissfully chilly basement and sort of play, but not really exert yourself.

I was also hanging with Heather's son a few days ago, and he was telling me about something he recently did at a nature camp: slug races! All you need is a few slugs (we have plenty), a hula hoop and some time. Pick a slug, put it in the middle of the hoop on the ground (substitute a chalk circle on pavement, or make a circle with string if you're not a hula-hooping fiend as we are in this family). And then just watch and cheer and wait for your slug to crawl to victory!

Next up? We're setting up a stake-out to spy those ravenous birds that have been eating all of our blueberries. We won't really take them out. We'll just give them friendly encouragement to move on. Maybe force them to listen to really bad loud music. You know. CIA tactics.

What are your favorite outdoor kid activities so far this summer?

Sunday Parkways: Let's ride! (or walk! or hoop!)

Yesterday, our family participated in the first Portland Sunday Parkways event we could fit in this summer, and it was awesome. Glorious sunny day. So many families out biking, running, roller skating, strolling babies, skate-boarding, or just plain old walking.



We've always used the occasion to bike ride, as it gives our young riders a chance to safely practice their still developing riding skills. There weren't that many sudden stops that nearly caused a pile-up among the riders behind us. But I sure did witness and experience a few of those and even caught myself blurting: 'Hey kid! You can't just stop in the middle of the road!' Learning experiences, with no blood lost. Yet, I need to remember that wheels are not required. It'd be just as fun to show up in some running or walking shoes, and take Sunday Parkways at a slower pace.

For the kids, the music and playgrounds and park activities are probably the best part. Not to mention the VooDoo donuts.



Me? I just like riding along, feeling the wind on my arms. Checking out electric bikes up close. Seeing different neighborhoods of our great city with little gardens everywhere. Being a part of the community of family friendly Portland bike culture. Observing all the bike boulevard and bike lane improvements that happen in a year's time. Seeing the friendly smiling faces of the Portland police as they manage the dicier intersections, and getting the chance to thank them and the countless volunteers who make these events possible.

There's three more Sunday Parkways coming this summer. If you can, put on your really flattering bike shorts (ha, ha!) and ride! Or walk, or hoop or what-have-you. Just get outside. Good times!

I want to live in a Playborhood


The other night we walked the kids down the road to a neighbor's house and played 3 on 3 basketball in the street. Yesterday some friends and their kids walked by our house and a spontaneous playdate / Kung Fu demo erupted in our driveway. I want more, more, more of this and I know other families in our neighborhood want it, too. More kids on bikes and scooters in the street. More baseball games in the backyard. I want to hear the voices of neighborhood children whooping it up as they chase each other around the yard with squirt guns. I've just stumbled onto a Web site that encourages this kind of behavior: Playborhood. It's inspiring, it's encouraging, it's the neighborhood I want for my kids. And check out this article about a neighborhood summer camp (inspired by Playborhood) that is just so freakin' cool! C'mon summer, bring out the kids!
