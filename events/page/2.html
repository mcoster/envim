A free class for Portland parents who want to green up their schools

The Center for Earth Leadership is offering a five-session class through its Eco-School Network for parents  who want to bring Earth-conscious practices into their schools. The class, How to be an Agent of Change in Your Circle of Influence, will have a focus on schools and how each individual can make a difference. You'll connect with other like-minded parents and work together to form strategies and plans for change, as well as take early steps toward implementation. The classes will be held on five Mondays: Oct. 18, 25, Nov. 1,8 and Dec. 6 from noon to 1:45pm. There is no charge for the class, but advanced registration is required. Contact Emily Klavins at 503-227-2315 to register. The Center is located at 319 SW Washington.

Portland: Muddy Boot Festival, Sept. 10-12

Mark your calendars, Portland peeps, for this year's Muddy Boot Festival! I've attended the past two years and love this event. Their mission: to promote sustainable living. Yee-haw! Firstly, those two high fructose corn-drinkin' boys from the movie King Corn, Ian Cheney and Curt Ellis, will be giving the keynote address on Friday the 10th at 7pm (advance tix are $15-20...totally worth it). The festival itself runs Saturday from noon-9pm and Sunday noon-6pm, $5 admission each day for ages 12 and up. You'll get great live music, sustainably-produced food and beverages, info booths and vendors selling green goods, workshops, discussion panels and fun activities for the kiddos. Personally, the backyard goats and the edible landscaping workshops on Saturday look awesome (and they are free with admission). It's all happening at St. Philip Neri Church at 2408 SE 16th at Division. Check it out and have fun!

Portland: be part of the record-breaking simultaneous breastfeeding event on August 6

Whew, that was kind of a mouthful. (Drum beat)

Update: I originally had this listed as August 16 -- it is actually August 6. Sorry!

No seriously, this is pretty cool. On Friday, August 6, breastfeeding moms and their babes will come together at locations all around Portland to attempt to break New Zealand's record for most mom's nursing simultaneously in one city. (New Zealand, population 4,268,900, had 1299 moms participate.) But in the grander scheme of things, Portland's Big Latch-Onis raising awareness of the importance of breastfeeding and helping nursing moms become more comfortable with nursing in general. To take part, go to one of the host venues by 10am to register and become a part of breastfeeding history! Venues include the Westside Academy of Kung Fu, Zenana Spa, Milagros Boutique, Bella Stella and several other locations.

May 22: Take our (free-range) children to the park and leave them there day


Since reading 'Free-Range Kids' a few months back, I've been letting my kids go off-leash and trying to give my helicoptering parenting style the pink slip with some successes and some failures to report. My older daughter has been walking to a neighbor's for Spanish class every Monday for weeks now. I no longer make her call me to say she's safely arrived after the 3 minute walk. Success! Then there was the day she and her amigos decided to play for 30 extra minutes after class. Without calling home. It was the longest 30 minutes of my life. Now, a normal person might have picked up the phone and called the host mom and said, 'Is my daughter still there?' Instead, I clutched my frantically beating heart after I could no longer wait a single second more and ran the distance between our houses, scanning the side of the road looking for signs of her abduction. I arrived at the house moments later, having found NOT A SINGLE CLUE, and was greeted with entirely reasonable apologies about the time just getting away from everyone and for not calling. My daughter now unfailingly calls when class goes over time.

There are some days when I'm more brave about free-ranging the kids. And other days when I just feel I can't give up the worry. But more often than not, I find myself asking: What would Lenore Skenazy do? Do I really have to be so freaked-out worried about everything? Mostly, the answer is no. And we encourage the kids to continue down the free-range road.

So here's a real free-range kids challenge. This Saturday, May 22. It's called: Take Our Children to the Park & Leave Them There Day. Now, this isn't for babies. It's for kids ages 7 and up, who might benefit and learn from some outside play time without parental supervision. The recommended start time is 10am. Thankfully, we have a totally open weekend. I'm not sure I'll be able to go all-out free-range -- but we do have a little neighborhood park near our library. Perhaps, I could go into the library while I trust that my 8 year old can take care of herself in the park across the street (And hopefully run into a free-range friend or two) while I browse for books. The library has big windows that face the park. And it's forecast to rain, so we don't have to make it an all-day affair. Baby steps.

Are you in? Are your horrified? Are you speed-dialing child protective services? What was your relationship with playing alone at the park when you were 7 or older? (the picture above is me and my brother, clearly under the age of 7, but we had a fairly free-range childhood and lived to tell).

Beaverton: backyard chicken town hall, June 8

May is Walk + Bike Month / Transportation Pyramid / Portland Sunday Parkways

The Oregon Safe Routes to School promotes May as Walk + Bike (to school) Challenge Month. We usually participate by walking most mornings when the sun shines. On those rainy mornings, I'm usually cursing the fact that I never really managed to get quality rain gear that fits the kids, and expected them to limp along in water repellent wind breakers and hope for the best. You'll also find me muttering under my breath: 'April showers bring May flowersâ€¦ So quit raining already, and bring on the blooms!'

Participating schools get cute little score cards where kids dig checking off the days they participate, by marking the appropriate icon: foot, bike or skateboard:

One of the goals of the program is to promote walking and biking for life. The brisk morning walk seems to wake up my kids' brains. Mine too.

Of course, walking and biking is not just for kids. Here's a great little concept from Portland's bureau of transportation: The Transportation Pyramid! Just like the Food Pyramid, only better for you!!


Your car is like the fats and sweets -- use only once in a while! The bases? Walk + Bike! Get 5-9 servings each of those every day. OK, I just make that part up. Love it!!

One last thing while we're talking about healthy walking and biking stuff. Portland's got FIVE, count 'em, FIVE Sunday Parkways events planned for this year, starting on May 16, and going through September. Keep your fingers crossed that the whole April showers thing will be done by May 16! Or just be tough like real Oregonians and bike/hike in the rain. We've tested this a few times already this year, and have gotten caught in some downpours. Although our little girls are assuredly made of sugar and spice and everything nice, I'm happy to report neither of them melted (or melted down!) in the rain.

Portland Earth Week events, April 22 & 24

Thursday is officially Earth Day (but doesn't every day feel like Earth Day?) and there are lots of activities and celebrations happening all over the Portland metro area on both Thursday and Saturday. Here are a few:

Thursday

St. Johns Earth Day Celebration, 2-7pm. A family event that focuses on how the Port of Portland protects the environment. Mad Science will offer zany, hands-on science experiments and the Burgerville truck will be hawking its mostly-local fast food in the parking lot. Free. 8427 N. Central

Garden Home Earth Day Sustainability Fair, 4-6pm. Check out this how-to sustainability fair featuring gardening, recycling, green building, kids crafts and more. Free. 7475 SW Oleson Road

Clackamas County Celebrating Earth Day Shred-it Day, 12-5pm. Paper shredding and electronics recycling available, including cell phones, hearing aids and eye glasses. Experts on hand to answer your recycling questions! Free shredding for 1st box of paper, $5 for each additional box. 694 NE Fourth, Canby.

Saturday

Earth Day 2010: Re-Seeding Our World!10am-7pm. City Repair presents this 11th annual event focused on community empowerment and sustainability. Music (featuring the awesome MarchFourth Marching Band @ 2:30), workshops, and info booths abound! Free. Washington HS Field, SE 12th & Stark

Earth Day Children's Book Drive, 10am-4pm. Drop off your gently-used children's books at any New Season's Market store or the Laurelhurst Whole Foods. All books will go to the Children's Book Bank, which will get them into the hands of low-income children in the Portland area.

Portland Farmers Market Earth Day Celebration, 9am-2pm. Along with the usual cornucopia of locally-grown offerings, enjoy Earth Day activities and DIY projects. Free. South Park Blocks between Hall & Montgomery

Hillsboro Earth Day Recycling Event, 9am-Noon. In conjunction with the Relay for Life, recycle electronics, bicycles, fluorescent light bulbs, rimless tires, and used clothing and household goods for reuse. $2 for tires, otherwise free. 2025 NW Cornelius Pass Road.

Earth Day Family Fun Fair, 12-5pm. Offerings include a native plant sale, children's crafts, CSA information, free garden seedlings for children, a maypole dance, and music. Master Recyclers (woo hoo!) will be collecting electronics, CFLs, household batteries, block styrofoam and old child car seats. Unitarian Universalist Church, 22785 NW Birch, Hillsboro.

Milagros Boutique Earth Day Swap, 2pm. Bring in household items, decor, books and magazines, clothing and anything else swap-able! Free. 5433 NE 30th.

Premier of Fresh the Movie, 1pm. Described by sustainable farming guru and film subject Joel Salatin as 'part 2 of Food Inc' this documentary explores more of America's need to reinvent its farming system. Runs through April 28, various times. Hollywood Theatre.

Feel free to add more Earth Day events in the comments!
